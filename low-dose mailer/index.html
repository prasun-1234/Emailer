Coronary Artery Calcium (CAC) Scoring
Low-dose CT can detect and quantify calcium deposits in the coronary arteries. This CAC score is a strong predictor of future heart attacks and other cardiovascular events. Smokers are at higher risk for coronary artery disease, making this information particularly relevant.
Aortic Calcification
The aorta is the main artery carrying blood from the heart. Calcium buildup in the aorta can indicate atherosclerosis (hardening of the arteries), another risk factor for heart disease and stroke.
Early signs of heart disease
CT scans can sometimes reveal subtle changes in the heart's structure or function, such as thickening of the heart walls or early signs of heart failure, that may not yet be causing noticeable symptoms.
